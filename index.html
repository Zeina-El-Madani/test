About Me

I am a Digital Media Studies and Business Marketing student at the University of Rochester, deeply passionate about filmmaking and cinema as tools to fight against injustice. I aspire to be a documentary videographer promoting Middle Eastern culture and rich history to raise awareness and change narratives in western countries.

Sumud, the title of my ongoing project, is an Arabic word that reflects the spirit of resilience. A word I didn't know what it truly meant until I was met with one of the forces it fights against: Censorship.

My Mission

Using storytelling as a tool for change, raising awareness, and fighting for justice through documentary filmmaking and photo-documentaries.

My Focus

Celebrating resilience of communities, advocating for them, and promoting their culture, traditions and identity through visual storytelling.

Featured Projects

Silenced Voices: Censorship & Resilience

An interactive experience and silent short film exploring the hardships journalists face during wartime and how censorship affects their work. This project uses metaphor to highlight the impact of censorship on Palestinian communities and the spirit of Sumud (resilience) that fights against oppression.

Documentary Interactive Story Social Justice Cultural Preservation
Explore Project

EZPark Website Design

EZPark is an app idea aiming to inform users regarding different open parking spots on campus which can allow students to decrease time looking for open spots on campus and also help to reduce traffic congestion. My contribution to the project was the Website design, focusing on user experience and accessibility.

Subject vs Seasons

A photographic exploration of how individuals react to and interact with different seasonal environments. This project captures the essence and aura of people within their surroundings, making viewers feel the emotional impact each season evokes. Through this series, I explored narrative storytelling through still imagery and environmental portraiture.
